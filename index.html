About Kuthampully

Handloom village

Most of the inhabitants belong to the Devanga community. They trace their origins to modern-day Karnataka. Legend has it that this community of traditional weavers was brought here by the Kochi royal family some 500 years back to ply their trade exclusively for the palace.

Kuthampully Saree is a type of Sari traditionally made by weavers from Kuthampully village in Thiruvilwamala Grama Panchayat of Thrissur district of Kerala state in India. The Kuthampully Saree is distinguished by its Saree borders.

About Handloom

The Warp and Weft of Tradition

Kerala's handloom products are well known for their intricate designs, exquisite craftsmanship, vibrant colours and delicate texture. For centuries, a majority of the product has been made families of weavers, each with a unique story on how they were introduced to this special trade.

Edmund Cartwright built and patented a power loom in 1785, and it was this that was adopted by the nascent cotton industry in England. The silk loom made by Jacques Vaucanson in 1745 operated on the same principles but was not developed further.
